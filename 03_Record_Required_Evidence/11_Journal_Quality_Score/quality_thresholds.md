# Quality Score Thresholds

## Important caution

This is a **Journal of Finance-style internal benchmark**, not an official score, not an acceptance prediction, and not a substitute for human research judgment.

## Idea-stage potential ceiling

| Score | Meaning | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 95-100 | Rare high-potential idea | Chase if feasible. Run literature, data, and design checks immediately. |
| 85-94 | Promising idea | Refine and stress-test before committing major effort. |
| 71-84 | Borderline idea | Continue only if there is a special reason: unique data, strategic value, teaching value, or strong human intuition. |
| 70 or below | Low ceiling | Give up, pivot, or merge into another idea unless the human explicitly overrides. |

## Final-paper score

| Score | Meaning | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 95-100 | Stop-revision zone if no hard-stop issues remain | Prepare final package and documentation. |
| 90-94 | Very strong but not done | Run targeted revision on the weakest dimensions. |
| 80-89 | Potentially serious paper, major gaps remain | Run another quality cycle or reconsider design/writing. |
| 70-79 | Weak for top finance audience | Rebuild contribution, design, data, or evidence. |
| Below 70 | Not ready | Stop, pivot, or redefine target. |

## Hard-stop issues

A hard-stop issue blocks readiness even if the score is above 95.

- data permission unclear;
- confidential/licensed data handled incorrectly;
- key citation invented or unverified;
- main result cannot be reproduced;
- causal claim unsupported by design;
- novelty is asserted but not positioned or tested;
- paper contradicts itself across abstract, intro, tables, and conclusion;
- required AFA-style reporting records are missing.
