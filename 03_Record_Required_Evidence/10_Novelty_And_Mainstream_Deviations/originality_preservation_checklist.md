# Originality Preservation Checklist

Use this checklist before the agent finalizes any stage where the contribution may be novel, unconventional, or outside the mainstream literature.

## Do not normalize too early

- [ ] Did the agent preserve at least one non-mainstream option long enough to evaluate it?
- [ ] Did the agent avoid rejecting an idea only because it is not the standard framing?
- [ ] Did the agent distinguish **unsupported** from **non-mainstream but testable**?
- [ ] Did the agent avoid rewriting the paper into a generic safe literature extension?

## Justification ladder

The paper's deviation should have at least some of the following:

- [ ] conceptual mechanism;
- [ ] institutional detail;
- [ ] measurement improvement;
- [ ] overlooked data source or population;
- [ ] testable prediction;
- [ ] falsification or placebo test;
- [ ] reason mainstream evidence may not apply;
- [ ] careful boundary condition.

## Claim discipline

- [ ] Is the deviation stated as a hypothesis until evidence exists?
- [ ] Is the contribution bounded rather than universal?
- [ ] Are alternative explanations named?
- [ ] Are null or conflicting results allowed to matter?
- [ ] Is the writing strong enough to communicate novelty but cautious enough to be credible?

## Mainstream referee check

- [ ] What would a mainstream referee say is wrong?
- [ ] Which objection is valid and must change the paper?
- [ ] Which objection is a taste difference but not fatal?
- [ ] Which evidence would make the skeptical reader update?

## Completion rule

A novel or deviating idea can proceed only if it is classified as one of:

```text
well-justified and testable
promising but needs evidence
keep as bounded hypothesis
move to limitations/future work
drop or merge into mainstream framing
```
